## Introduction

Piracy in Star Citizen is one of the most demanding — and rewarding — gameplay loops in the 'verse. It requires the right gear, solid ship knowledge, good teamwork, and a lot of patience. This guide covers everything you need to get started: what to bring, which ships to fly, how an operation actually unfolds, what loot to prioritize, and where to dock when you're wanted.

A word of warning before we begin: **piracy is 90% waiting and 10% action.** If you're expecting non-stop combat, you'll be disappointed. If you're willing to play the long game, the payoff is worth it.

---

## Gear

Having the right kit before you undock is non-negotiable. Here's what every boarding crew member should be carrying:

- **Primary weapon** — something effective in close quarters. The Ripper is a strong recommendation. Leave the sniper rifle at home.
- **Rail gun** — essential for breaching ship doors since the armor changes. Carry 2–3 magazines. Avoid the Boom Tube; it one-shots smaller ships too easily and risks destroying what you came to take.
- **Multi-tool with Tractor Beam and Combio SRT attachment** (with canister) — more on how this is used in the boarding section.
- **Fire extinguisher** — enables fast EVA movement when sneaking up on a disabled ship. Essential for solo approaches.
- **Backpack large enough to hold your primary weapon** — so you can switch between your primary and the fire extinguisher mid-approach without dropping either.
- **Helmet with AR Vision** — close-quarters boarding almost always involves hip-fire. The AR overlay gives you a meaningful edge.

## Ships

Piracy is a multi-role operation. You'll need ships filling each of these roles depending on group size.

### Tackle (Quantum Interdiction)
- **RSI Mantis** — the only ship in the game with both a QID and QED. S3 QED (20 km snare, 12 km dampener). Required for group operations.

### Dampener / Support Tackle
- **Drake Cutlass Blue** — S1 QD, 4 km dampener
- **Mirai Guardian QI** — S3 QD, 4.5 km dampener
- **RSI Scorpius Antares** — S3 QD, 5 km dampener

### Fighters
Fly whatever suits your style — you're a pirate. That said, bring something useful. The goal is disabling ships, not destroying them.

- **Razor EX** — a strong solo choice thanks to its stealth profile; good for sneaking up on targets

### Cargo Recovery
You need something with enough hold to haul the take. The **Hull B** is a solid current option. The **Iron Clad** (when released) will be worth considering — each has trade-offs.

### Heavy Deterrent
- **Polaris / Idris** — reserved for situations where a target's org shows up to contest the operation. Not standard kit, but good to have on standby for large ops.

## QID vs. QED — Know the Difference

This is one of the most important distinctions in piracy and it's worth calling out explicitly:

> **QID (Quantum Interdiction Device):** Pulls a ship out of active quantum travel. The Mantis is currently the only ship equipped with one.
>
> **QED (Quantum Enforcement Device):** Prevents a ship from *entering* quantum travel. It also prevents NAV speeds. Note — it does not stop a ship from toggling NAV mode, but if they try, they'll stay locked at SCM speeds and lose their shields. That's a win for you.

These are two separate systems with two separate purposes. The Mantis has both. Every other tackle ship listed above carries only a QED.

## Scenarios

No two operations in Star Citizen play out the same way, but most piracy runs fall into one of the following patterns.

### Scenario 1 — Group Cargo Interdiction (Recommended for New Players)

This is the standard FSS piracy operation and the best entry point if you're new to the loop.

**Setup:**
Operations are typically run out of the **Nyx system**, between Levski and the Stanton gate. To calculate where to drop out of quantum for an optimal snare position, use [snareplan.dolus.eu](https://snareplan.dolus.eu/) — plug in your variables and it will tell you exactly where to position. The math is complex under the hood, but the tool handles it.

**The wait:**
Once in position, activate the QID and let it charge fully. Then you wait. There is no notification when a ship drops out of quantum nearby — you need to be actively pinging at all times. This is the grind. Stay focused.

**Contact:**
The moment a target drops, immediately switch to the QED if you're running solo tackle. Your priority is preventing them from jumping back out before your fighters arrive. Keep the range data from the Ships section in mind — if a target is outside your dampener range, they're gone.

**Fighter role:**
Fighters have a straightforward job: disable the ship. Use distortion weapons or target the power plant and engines directly. The goal is a dead ship, not a dead crew — yet.

**Boarding:**
Once the ship is disabled, it's time to board. This is where your gear pays off.

Use the **rail gun** to breach the doors. Smaller ships like the Cutlass Black typically need one round. Larger ships like the M2 Hercules may take two or three. If your group is large enough, breach from multiple entry points simultaneously — it prevents the crew from funneling your party into a single chokepoint.

Once inside, clear the ship methodically. Move through, secure the crew, and take stock of what's there.

## Loot Priority

The temptation is to grab everything. Resist it. With the changes in patch 4.8, some categories are no longer worth the cargo space.

**Skip these:**
- **Ship components** — component bricking in 4.8 means stripped components have minimal resale value.
- **Contracted cargo** — flagged items don't sell reliably and aren't worth the risk.

**Take everything else.** Specific callouts:
- **Missiles** — with 4.8's economy changes, these are now a significant money-maker. Prioritize them.
- **Personal gear** — armor, weapons, and medical supplies from the crew all sell.

## Safe Havens

Operating with a crime stat limits where you can dock safely. Know your options before you need them.

- **Grim HEX** (Stanton) — the primary safe haven for wanted players in Stanton. Always reliable.
- **Nyx System stations** — currently monitored. Not safe if you're carrying a crime stat.
- **Pyro** — viable throughout the system if Stanton gets too hot.

When running a full operation, plan your post-op route before you undock. Getting caught at a station with a hull full of stolen cargo ends the night early.
